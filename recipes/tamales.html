What is a Tamale

A tamale is a traditional Meso american dish made of filled masa dough steamed in a corn husk. Masa is a maize dough made from ground nixtamalized corn.

What are Tamales made of?

These tamales consist of a seasoned pork filling in masa harina dough wrapped in dried corn husks. The finished tamals are topped with a homemade chile sauce.

Ingredients

Filling:

  • 1 1/4 pounds of pork loin
  • 1 large onion, halved
  • 1 clove of garlic

Chile Sauce:

  • 4 dried chile peppers
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons of salt

Husks and Dough:

  • 1(8 ounce) package of dried corn husks
  • 2/3 cup of lard
  • 1(10.5 ounce) can beef broth, or as needed
  • 2 cups of masa harina
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup sour cream

Directions

  1. Step 1

    Make the filling: Place pork, onion, and garlic in a Dutch oven. Add enough water to cover and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to love and simmer until pork is cook through, about 2 hours.

  2. Step 2

    Meanwhile, make the chile sauce: Use rubber gloves to remove stems and seeds from chiles. Place chiles in a saucepan with 2 cup of water. Simmer, uncovered, for 20 minutes. Remove from heat and cool.

  3. Step 3

    Transfer chiles and cooking water to a blender and blend until smooth. Strain mixture into a bowl and stir in salt. Set aside.

  4. Step 4

    Shred cooked pork and mix in 1 cup of chile sauce. Reserve the remaining sauce for serving.

  5. Step 5

    Prepare husks and dough: Soak corn husks in a bowl of warm water until softened, about 30 minutes.

  6. Step 6

    While the husks are soaking, beat lard with 1 tablespoon of broth until fluffy. Combine masa harina, baking powder, and salt in a separate bowl: stir into the lard mixture, adding more broth as needed to form a spongy dough.

  7. Step 7

    Remove husks from water and pat dry. Spread dough out over husk to 1/4-to-1/2-inch thick.

  8. Step 8

    Place 1 tablespoon pork filling over center of each one. Fold the sides, top, and bottom of each husk in toward the center to enclose dough.

  9. Step 9

    Arrange tamales in a steamer basket. Place over simmering water and steam for 1 hour.

  10. Step 10

    Remove tamales from husks. Drizzle with remaining chile sauce and top with sour cream, or mix sour cream into chile sauce first for a creamy sauce.
